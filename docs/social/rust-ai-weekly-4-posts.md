## LinkedIn

The rewrite just became a budget line.

Bun's creator published the story of porting the entire runtime to Rust with AI doing the heavy lifting: roughly $165k of model usage at API rates. Microsoft shipped the Go-powered TypeScript 7 compiler the same week. The leadership question has quietly inverted: it is no longer "can we afford the rewrite" but "which target language leaves us maintainable after the machine writes it".

That thread runs through Rust & AI Weekly #4, out today. 42 crates and tools mapped by verdict (Adopt / Trial / Assess / Hold), each with maintenance, release, and adoption signals:

◆ copper-rs — Guillaume Binet's deterministic robotics runtime went 1.0 after 1043 pull requests. If your AI roadmap includes anything embodied, this is the substrate to evaluate first.
◆ rama — five years of work behind the network framework powering AI proxy gateways; 0.3 ships with a release-train promise worth watching.
◆ apalis — Go-land is talking about River for Postgres-backed jobs; apalis is the Rust seat at that table, nearing 1.0.
◆ GuardianDB — third mention on the radar, now speaking the PostgreSQL wire protocol. The verdict arc is the story.

Also in the issue: Rust 1.97.0's v0 symbol mangling, an RFC to move registry tokens into the OS credential store, and why the Rust answer to a trending tool often predates the question.
